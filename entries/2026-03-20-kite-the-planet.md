## 1. Problem Statement

KTP lacked formal documentation of its competitive position, growth trajectory, and brand rationale. Strategic thinking was happening in conversation but not persisted — no reference documents for investor conversations, co-founder onboarding, or future-session continuity.

## 2. Solution

Built two documents in `docs/competitive/`:

**`growth-projections.md`** — 5-year, 20-quarter MRR model across four scenario tracks (Very Conservative → Aggressive). Grounded in KTP-specific factors rather than generic SaaS curves. Opens with a frank assessment of 10 growth contributors and 12 growth hindrances before the numbers appear. Year 5 ARR range: $192K (very conservative) to $7.2M (aggressive). Each scenario includes a narrative covering what goes right, what goes wrong, and the Viking/Yoplanning/Ridesk competitive dynamics specific to that path.

**`brand-positioning.md`** — Linguistic and strategic analysis of the "Kite the Planet" brand and `kitetheplanet.com` domain. Covers: hard consonant memorability (K-factor), "kite" as a universal loanword already owned by the community in every language, "planet" cognate coverage across European and Latin American markets, honest weaknesses (three words, mild cliché energy in English), competitor brand comparison table, and a corrected domain valuation ($5–30K unbuilt standalone, not $50–150K).

## 3. Lessons Learned

- **Grade on the right curve.** A brand rating is only meaningful relative to a named comparison set.
- **Don't confuse domain value with acquisition value.** Unbuilt domain ≠ going-concern business.
- **The leading indicator is behavioral, not financial.** Viking schools asking Viking for a KTP integration is the signal. MRR is lagging.
- **Cold-start documentation is strategy.** The artifact makes the argument coherent to a future partner, investor, or hire.
